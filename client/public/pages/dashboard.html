Information

The hip throw, also known as the hip toss, is another fundamental takedown in grappling and is great for self-defense. The hip throw is a technique that is often first learned by beginners. The goal of this is to unbalance the opponent and their footwork and get underneath the opponent's center of gravity. There are many variations that can be adapted from this technique, but it is important to master down the basic principles of it first.

The main principle behind the hip throw is the setup. Typically, you want the opponent’s hips back while the opponent is leaning forward. This allows you to position your hip underneath the opponent and pull them into the hip throw.

When finishing the Hip Throw make sure to keep the opponent’s near arm trapped to pin them to the mat. To avoid getting rolled through, keep your hips low to the mat, and remain in control of your opponent.
